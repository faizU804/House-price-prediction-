Our Model Architecture

We utilize an ensemble approach to ensure robust predictions across all Melbourne regions.

1. Gradient Boosting

Our primary engine. It sequentially optimizes regression trees, focusing on "hard-to-predict" outliers in high-volatility suburbs like Richmond and South Yarra.

2. Feature Engineering

We employ high-dimension One-Hot Encoding for 345+ suburbs and Council Areas, ensuring that hyperlocal demand signals are never lost in the noise.

3. Data Imputation

Using median strategy imputation, we ensure that incomplete historical records (e.g., missing landsize) don't compromise the final prediction integrity.

We Use a **Train-Test Split** methodology. 80% of data is used for learning, while 20% is held back for blind testing. The current R² score of 0.825 means our AI can explain over 82% of the variance in Melbourne property prices.

Real estate pricing is non-linear. Linear regression fails to capture the "scarcity premium" of certain suburbs or the exponential impact of adding a third bathroom. Gradient Boosting handles these complexities with ease.

Our current model focuses on structural and geospatial value. Macroeconomic factors like interest rate shifts are indirectly captured through the historical transaction price sensitivity in our training set.
